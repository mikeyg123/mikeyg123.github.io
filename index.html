Calendar Test


This is a shared google calendar embedded into another web page:



To create this I logged into the google calendar website, make a new calendar, make it public in the settings and copy the links into this web-page.
The size and some of the colours can be modified by changing the iframe src link

Subscribe to this calendar

Anyone can click here to subscribe to public events on their phone, computer, outlook etc.
This is a read-only view that should work with any smartphone

To unsubscribe:
On an iphone go to Settings -> Calendar -> Accounts -> Subscribed Calendars; and delete "WBKA"
Android and other apps will have a similar setting somewhere.

To manage events:

Admins need a google login and then the calendar can be shared with them.
Once this is done they can either use the google calendar website or add their google login to their smartphone calendar settings.
Depending on the permission they are given they can view or manage events and control who else has what permission

I've tested this with apple's calendar app on the iphone and with google's android app and it works pretty well.
The key thing to remember to set the correct calendar name when you create a new event or it just goes into your personal diary.

As far as I can tell there's no primary owner of a shared calendar. Everyone with "make changes and manage sharing" permission has equal control and can remove other people.
I expect only a couple of people should have this power to reduce the chance of someone messing it up.
